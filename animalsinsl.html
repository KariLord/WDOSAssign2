WILDLIFE IN SRI LANKA

Indigeonous Animals in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka, an island nation in South Asia, is home to a rich diversity of indigenous flora and fauna. The country's varied ecosystems, including rainforests, dry zones, and coastal areas, contribute to the existence of a wide range of animal species. Some places in which These animals can be seen is Yala National Park and Wilpattu National Park

Asian Elephant

The Sri Lankan elephant is a subspecies of the Asian elephant and is considered an iconic species in the country. These elephants can be found in various national parks and wildlife reserves.

Sri Lankan Leopard

The Sri Lankan leopard is a leopard subspecies native to Sri Lanka and is known for its distinctive dark golden or rufous coat. It is the island's top predator and can be found in protected areas.

Sloth Bear

The Sri Lankan sloth bear is a subspecies of the sloth bear found in the island's dry zone. These bears are known for their shaggy coat and are primarily nocturnal.

Purple-faced Langur

Also known as the purple-faced leaf monkey, this primate species is endemic to Sri Lanka. It is characterized by its dark face and is often found in the country's rainforests.

Toque Macaque

Another primate endemic to Sri Lanka, the toque macaque is recognized by its tufted crown of hair. It inhabits various habitats, including forests, urban areas, and agricultural landscapes.

Red Slender Loris

This nocturnal primate is native to Sri Lanka and is known for its large, round eyes. Unfortunately, it is classified as a vulnerable species due to habitat loss and other threats.
